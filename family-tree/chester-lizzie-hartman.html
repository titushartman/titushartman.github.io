The April 9, 1917 edition of the Reading Eagle newspaper reported that Chester HARTMAN of Boyertown and Lizzie HAUSMAN of Gabelsville had been married at Elkton, Maryland.
Chester registered for the military draft on Jun 5, 1917 at age 21. This record indicates that he resided with Lizzie on West Philadelphia Avenue and was employed as a farm hand in Boyertown by James B. Stauffer.
On October 2, 1923, Chester Hartman filed an application for a US patent on a faucet. The corresponding patent US 1,496,986 was issued on June 10, 1924.
The March 21, 1924 edition of the Reading Eagle newspaper reported that Chester's twin brother Lester had been burned in a gasoline fire at Chester's auto repair garage.

On July 30 of the following year, the Reading Eagle reported that the fixtures of Chester's garage were to be sold to another business.
Chester registered again for the military draft on April 27, 1942 at age 46. At this time, he and his family resided at the rear unit of 33 South Reading Avenue, and the record indicates that he was employed at the rear unit of 22 North Reading Avenue. Chester registered this time as a conscientious objector.
In their later years, Chester and Lizzie lived at 120 North Reading Street in Boyertown. Chester and Titus HARTMAN are pictured in the back yard of this home during the early 1950s.
Chester and Lizzie are pictured in their home and on the occasion of their 50th wedding anniversary.
